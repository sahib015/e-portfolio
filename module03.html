Module 03- Security and Risk Management

Learning Outcomes
  • Review a number of common definitions of Security and Risk Management.
  • Review some common Security and Risk Management Concepts.
  • Discuss the Risk Management Process.
  • Review the Risk Management Process (RMP).
  • Review the effects of different assessment types (Qualitative vs. Quantitative).
  • Discuss potential changes to the RMP based on changes in user participation.
  • Discuss the difference between threats and vulnerabilities.
  • Review a number of threat modelling techniques.
  • Discuss a number of guides and cookbooks to help with threat modelling.
  • Discuss various tools and resources that can be used in threat modelling and management.
  • Evaluate the use of publicly available tools to assist with Threat Modelling.
  • Create threat models based on suggested scenarios.
  • Explore industry specific security standards and directives.
  • Discuss how standards affect security and risk assessments.
  • Review a number of common industry and enterprise standards.
  • Review and discuss a number of GDPR related case studies.
  • Review standards web sites.
  • Discuss a number of case studies and standards.
  • Discuss what is meant by quantitative risk modelling (QRM).
  • Review different approaches to QRM including Monte Carlo Simulations and Bayes theoretical models.
  • Discuss the principles and antipatterns for each approach.
  • Discuss how QRM can be applied to real world problems.
  • Review case studies of how QRM techniques are used.
  • Provide a critical evaluation of some of the techniques available.
  • Review how and why to create business continuity (BC) and disaster recovery (DR) plans.
  • Discuss the main determining factors in BC/DR plans – including Business Impact Assessments (BIA), Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs) and Recovery Point Objectives (RPOs).
  • Discuss emerging trends in Information Risk Management.
  • Discuss the impact of RPO and RTO values on DR solutions.
  • Examine some typical system solutions to meet the various standby requirements.
  • Describe the limitations of the proposed solutions.
  • Review a number of current and emerging trends in SRM.
  • Discuss the pros and cons of each trend.
  • Evaluate each trend in respect of its influence on future directions.
  • Debate the question, split into two opposing ‘teams’.
  • Complete the wiki entries for the future trends reading.
  • Vote and decide the most influential trend.

For this activity I chose the case DATA BREACH AT AN ONLINE RETAILER (2016)

From the case scenario, it was derived that personal data was stolen at the online retailer without the customers consent. This was resolved by the following:

  • Contracts are now in place to ensure the appropriate technical security
  • Conducts regular reviews of server and website
  • Conducts annual reviuews by 3rd party experts ensuring compliance and to independently validate that the appropriate technical security and organisational measures are in place

The steps that I would have taken if this was my organisation and as an Information Secuirty Manager to mitigate this are:

  • Ensure a firewall is installed and configured correctly
  • Daily and regular backup of data
  • Ensure the payment gateway is secured
  • Enable 2 factor authentication on the website
  • Ensure and review the policies are up to date with the GDPR, ISO and PCI regular standards

    How do Goerlandt et al (2017) suggest that the validity of QRA approaches can be validated? What did they posit was the most effective approach?

    Goerlandt et al (2017) suggests 3 approaches that the validity of QRA can be validated. These include, conceptional validity, validity in relation
    to different scientific foundations and pragmatic validity. The authors also suggested that for establishing pragmatic validity of risk analysis,
    the following approaches: complete benchmark exercise, partial benchmark exercise, reality check, independent review, and quality assurance.

    Which techniques did Hugo et al (2018) should be applied to project management? What were their recommendations to increase the use of QR analysis in Projects?

    After reading Hugo et al (2018), the techniques should be applied to project management are risk management tools. These techniques assist in project
    performance as the tool links the different phases during the project life cycle. There are several factors that may affect the use of the risk management
    tools, some of these factors include:

    • Size of project in terms of the cost, duration, and resources.
    • Skill level, training and experience of the project and risk management staff.
    • Appropriate and accurate risk models
    • The quality of data used
    • Integration of risk management with project management practice

    Their recommendations to increase the use of QR analysis in projects include,

    • Have a structured interview approach with the risk personnel to understand specific issues
    • Perform trials with software products to understand better
    • Improve individuals risk management competence from training and exposure

    The last paper reviews various Multi-criteria decision methods (MCDMs) and considered the relative accuracy and validity of the techniques.
    Which did they find was the most accurate of the methods compared? What were the failings of the general TOPSIS approach?

    Most accurate methods,

    • AHP

      Fallings of the general TOPSIS approach include,

      • The rank reversal phenomenon
      • The ranking index
      • The correlation between criteria

              Part A

                Read Opara-Martins et al (2014) and Morrow et al (2021) and answer the following questions:

                • What are some of the main vendor lock-in issues the authors identify? How would you mitigate them?
                • What are some of the security concerns with the modern cloud? How can these be mitigated?

                What are some of the main vendor lock-in issues the authors identify? How would you mitigate them?

                The main vendor issues the authors identify are:

                • Each provider develops its own specific technologies, remote APIs and some create new programming languages.
                • Switching between incompatible software systems can force a customer to use the same products and services
                • Applications are usually restricted to particular enterprises cloud or a cloud service provider.
                • Businesses can face a lock-in situation making it difficult for them to change the service provider in the future

                How would you mitigate them?

                • Build portable application
                • Retain ownership of data
                • Implement a multi-cloud strategy
