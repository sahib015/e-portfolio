Module 01- Launching into Cybersecurity

Learning Outcomes

The learning outcomes for this module were

  • Understand the key concepts of Confidentiality
  • Integrity and Availability in Cyber Security.
  • Able to address Cyber Security issues, gain awareness of the ethical and governance.
  • Describe a selection of the attack surfaces in a network through considering the key physical technologies used in networked communication.
  • Developed an awareness of the implications of security breaches.
  • Develop an awareness of emerging trends in Cyber Security.
  • Develop knowledge about approaches to identify vulnerabilities and threats.
  • Evaluated available techniques and technologies at database and metadata levels dealing with privacy and data disclosure, and the implications of vulnerabilities and threats in software and networks.
  • Develop solutions that will efficiently identify vulnerabilities and threats in software and over networks.
  • Develop security requirement documents to ensure security is embedded in a software development process.
  • Develop the capacity to predict security issues in software and develop appropriate solutions.
  • Develop the ability to apply concepts and principles of secure object-oriented analysis and design.
  • Develop the knowledge and skills required for programming.
  • Develop the ability to implement a security designed solution.
  • Develop the ability to identify the software components and associated threats.
  • Employ the UML modelling techniques to identify software dataflow, storage and security issues.
  • Apply the knowledge and skills to other security issues in software development.

Collaborative Discussion 01

Topic: Discuss why Cyber Security is now a global issue and why it is important for companies to invest in Cyber Security.

Initial Post

Over the years, cybersecurity has been growing as an industry. The cyber risks have also increased over the years. In recent years, the growth of technology has accelerated due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The pandemic demonstrated the power of technology as organisations over the world were introducing the work from home model. However, this exposed organisations vulnerabilities and unpreparedness (These are the top cybersecurity challenges of 2021, 2022).

The digital world helps organisations and individuals connect with each other whereas, in developing countries, this creates opportunities to improve he technology in the sectors such as health (Cybersecurity risks are global. We must address them with a coordinated, collaborative approach, 2022).

Cyber-attacks over the years have increased significantly and affect clients in various ways. With the different types of cyber-attacks such as phishing attacks, ransomware attacks, data breaches, cybersecurity has become a global issue as organisations and individuals have embraced themselves with technology. Individuals have embraced technology as part of their daily routine with smart devices that are connected via the internet, using financial methods to make payments on a regular basis such as Apple Pay, PayPal, among others. On the other hand, companies have embraced technology by ensuring their software is up to date with the latest versions available.

It is important for companies to invest in cybersecurity as this will help the companies to mitigate the risk factor by having the right policies and procedures in place, be able to assess the level of risk at a given time and prioritise on areas that are of high risk in mitigating a cyber-attack in the organisation.

Collaborative Discussion 2

Topic: Identify and discuss two security technologies and the context in which they can be employed. Your discussion could consider either network or/and software security issues. For example, if a network issue, what type of firewall would be ideal for the solution? What are the strengths and weaknesses of the solution?

Initial Post

There are several technologies that can be employed to protect an organisation from cyberattacks however, I shall discuss two technologies that can be employed.

Data Loss Prevention

Data loss prevention also known as DLP ensures that sensitive data is not lost, misused, or accessed by any unauthorised personnel. (What is Data Loss Prevention (DLP)? A Definition of Data Loss Prevention, 2022). This technology is divided into two categories: Enterprise DLP and Integrated DLP. Integrated DLP is limited to email encryption, secure gateways used for emails and secure web gateways while enterprise DLP are packaged for agent software’s specifically for desktops and servers, physical and virtual appliances for monitoring networks of an organisation and email traffic for data discovery. (What Is DLP and How Does It Work? | McAfee, 2022).

Security Incident and Event Management System

This technology is involved in invoking alerts when unusual activities are found within the organisations network. This keeps a track of the logs while protecting the organisations network security is not compromised in real time. (Technologies, 2022). There are several advantages of an organisation to deploy a Security Incident and Event Management System (SIEM). The main advantages include.

  • Improves organisational efficiency
  • AI driven automation
  • Detection of advanced and unknown threats such as insider threats, phishing, SQL injections, Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS).
