Module 08- Research Methods and Professional Practice

Learning Outcomes

The learning outcomes for this module were

  1. Introduce the elements within the scientific method.
  2. Introduce the various aspects of ethical issues in research, and by extension, in professional practice.
  3. Look at how you formulate and revise research questions.
  4. Look at all the parts that make up a research proposal and how to present your thoughts.
  5. Explain what a literature review is, how to perform it and how to present it.
  6. Introduce the concept of exploratory and descriptive research designs.
  7. Introduce 3 research methods – quantitative, qualitative and mixed method research - as well as primary and secondary research methods, and the data collection methods associated with each one.
  8. Introduce you to case studies, focus groups and observation methods.
  9. Discuss the advantage and drawbacks of these methods.
  10. Consider the types of data you will obtain.
  11. Introduce you to interview methods and survey methods.
  12. Consider the definitions of population and sample.
  13. Introduce the concept of pre- and post-testing.
  14. Introduce the different methods of obtaining responses.
  15. Discuss how to improve the responses you obtain.
  16. Consider how you would analyse the responses from each type of question.
  17. Introduce the concepts of validity, generalisability and reliability and how they impact on your research design.
  18. Consider the differences between qualitative and quantitative data and how to analyse and present your results.
  19. Define the different levels of quantitative data.
  20. Define measures of location and spread.
  21. Introduce the concept of inference and hypothesis testing.
  22. Introduce the various ways to analyse, interpret and present results from qualitative data.
  23. Discuss the advantages and drawbacks of each of these methods.
  24. Understand how to structure a dissertation and how to prepare yourself for the writing.
  25. Review your learning approach based on your reflections on your learning process.
  26. Review the Professional Skills matrix, considering your professional goals and the outcomes of the modules you have undertaken in your programme.
  27. Define and explain the concept of project management.
  28. Introduce you to the concept of project life cycles and project methodologies.
  29. Explore how projects can be impacted by risk and uncertainty.
  30. Learn about the relationship between risks, assumptions and constraints and the threats and opportunities which risks can pose.
  31. Learn how to develop a risk management plan and change management process for projects

The usage of dark UX Patterns for manipulation, may be just an evolution of selling behaviour, that was previously seen in traditional brick-and-mortar stores personally. A study (Alawadhi et al, 2016) confirms that store layout and a perception of it being busy or crowded, help to reinforce to the shopper that it must be worth the effort to shop there. This modelling shows that manipulation as part of the buying experience is nothing new. Perhaps people in general, are far more easily manipulated than we would like to think.

The recent increase in online shopping practices changes the game a bit though. From an online shopping site perspective, and using an understanding of human psychology, a web page is designed to appeal more through whatever means – colours, shapes, Lay-out, etc, in order to try and convince the shopper to purchase more of the items.

The same practices used in real shops, are copied online, but with much more simplicity. Gray states (Gray et al, 2021) “We also rely upon recent work that describes the role of crafty psychological nudging through the incorporation of “dark patterns” and instances where coercion is blatantly described in ways that often force users to make decisions against their own best interest—what Gray and colleagues describe as “asshole designs””.Ì

What is also interesting in their study, is that their results show that a very high percentage of users (over 80%) mistrust smartphone applications or websites, at least some of the time, and not just related to shopping experiences. Collaborative Discussion 2

Topic: Case Study: Accuracy of information

Abi is a researcher at an institute and also a statistical programmer. Abi has received a project from a manufacturer to review the nutritional value of a new cereal, Whizzz. Having collected the necessary data, he now needs to perform the appropriate analyses and print the reports for him to send to the manufacturer. Unfortunately, the data Abi has collected seems to refute the claim that Whizzz is nutritious, and, in fact, they may indicate that Whizzz is harmful.

Abi also realises that some other correlations could be performed that would cast Whizzz in a more favourable light. “After all,” he thinks, “I can use statistics to support either side of any issue.”

Ethical Concerns

  1. Clearly, if Abi changed data values in this study he would be acting unethically. But is it any more ethical for him to suggest analysing correct data in a way that supports two or more different conclusions?
  2. Is Abi obligated to present both the positive and the negative analyses?
  3. Is Abi responsible for the use to which others put his program results?
  4. If Abi does put forward both sets of results to the manufacturer, he suspects that they will publicise only the positive ones. What other courses of action has he?

Initial Post

Having collected the necessary data, Abi should not manipulate the data to support a biased conclusion as this would be considered fraud. If Abi shares his findings that are biased and focuses on his conclusion of the analysis in a particular direction while omitting important information is unethical as this can mislead the buyer of the cereal (The National Academies Press, 2009).

Statistics during an analysis have an impact on the analysis when appropriately used. According to (Grilli, et al., 2023), there are tools and techniques for handling data that would allow data professionals like Abi to extract necessary information to provide insights. However, when statistics are used inappropriately, results can be tampered with leaving important information and misleading the buyer. Therefore, it is important that Abi is transparent and unbiased as this would ensure he completes the analysis ethically.

AAccording to (Simundic, 2013) it is dishonest and unethical to conduct biased research, and therefore, Abi should include the ethical concerns he has in his analysis.

Individual Reflection

During the last twelve weeks, I learnt about Research Methods and Professional Practice. This module covered the various aspects ranging from tools and techniques that would be used as a researcher.

I this module, there were different activities on a weekly basis that I was able to contribute such as the collaborative discussions, formative submissions, and compulsory e-portfolio activities.

The collaborative discussion topics were interesting and in particular I found the collaborative discussion about Code of Ethics and Professional Conduct interesting than the case study- Accuracy of Information. Prior to this module, I had the interpretation that code of ethics is a certain method to perform a task in the correct manner. The collaboration forum allowed me to broaden my knowledge by analysing a case study and with further reading I understood that there are different aspects to consider such as the legal and social issues. Additionally, there are two types of ethics to consider, and these are (1) Compliance Based and (2) Value based (HAYES, 2023).

I felt that this module was well structured in relation to the final decertation where various concepts learnt in this module can potentially help and guide me through the decertation. Some of the topics that I felt are relevant towards the decertation are (1) Understanding the types of reasoning (MIESSLER, 2018), (2) the different research designs (Question Pro, 2021) and (3) Questionnaire design to use to collect primary data of the research topic (Question Pro, 2020).

Working on the various formative submissions throughout the module, helped me build upon my criticality skills and to be able to critique various literature which helped me on my literature review.

The literature review summative was a great preparation for my final decertation as this gives me a rough idea on the expectations for the decertation. While working on the literature review, I understood the importance of having a brief outline to help in guiding through the relevant key points required within the literature review.

The feedback received from the tutor was informative and positive. From the feedback received, I felt that I could have done better in some areas such as reading more publications relevant towards the topic to potentially increase the criticality and as a result, there would be a debate within the literature. Additionally, I should mix up the citations instead of focussing on a particular author before going to the next. By mixing up the authors, I would be able to draw different opinions and build a debate. This was informative feedback as I can keep the points of improvement and use them for the final decertation.

The presentation summative, I found that it provides a method to share your topic in a summary with key important factors that you would want to share prior to the research along the methodology and timelines of the research. This provides a mode of communication to present to others and this can be applied in the real world by presenting to potential clients on a particular presentation.

The feedback shared by the tutor for the research presentation was informative with some great pointes to improve on. One of the pointers mentioned was for me to ensure that I need to work on the pace of the presentation to allow in depth analysis within a short period of time. Another area of improvement was for me to add more slides to the presentation as some areas during the presentation were too long and ensure to keep the audience in mind while presenting to ensure there is engagement during the presentation. These pointers do provide great areas to improve on for the future and can be applied in the future.

As part of the formative submissions was a statistical analysis where I was tasked to analyse set of statistics on an Excel workbook using Hypothesis and Bar Charts. I found analysing the data with bar charts easier compared to the hypothesis method as visuals are easier to interpret. I ensured that I was able to critically analyse the data and support my reasoning with the relevant references.

From the feedback received from the tutor, I was pleased with the feedback as I felt I did a great job critically analysing the data provided.

The professional skills matrix provides an overview of the various skills I am proficient and skills that I need to improve on. I feel this does benefit me as it gives an overview of the key skills required. This shoed me that I am proficient in areas such as teamwork and areas I need to build on such as commercial awareness.

I feel I lack commercial awareness in Cyber Security as I have changed my career path from physical security to Cyber Security. Prior to the slight change of career path, I was aware of the latest technology in relation to physical security. With Cyber Security, it is an evolving industry and new tools, technology, it is difficult to keep up on a regular basis however it is important to, and I plan on working towards keeping up with the current tools of the industry.

This module provided me with great insights on ways to conduct research that will be useful going forward in my career and the final decertation of my master’s degree.
